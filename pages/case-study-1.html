Context

THE BUSINESS OPPORTUNITY

DriveVoya is an app that incentivizes attentive driving by rewarding students and community members for staying off their phones while on the road. DriveVoya came to us with a beta version of their app and a simple, single page website. They needed a way to capture the attention of primary stakeholders in order to further develop their app.

THE PROCESS

  • ● Timeline: 3-week sprint
  • ● My Role: UX Designer; User Interviews, Usability Testing, Design Studio, Wireframing + Prototyping
  • ● Key Methods: Organizational research + user interviews, Data collection + interpretation, Competitive analysis, Sketching, Wireframing, Prototyping, Usability     testing, Iterating, High-fidelity wireframes
  • ● Tools: Pencil and paper, Communication: Slack, Collaboration: Google Drive, Wireframes: Sketch, Prototype: InVision

Domain Research

DriveVoya's target audience consists of businesses, students, educators, and parents/community members. Before doing our competitive analysis, I defined the following competitors:

  • ● Pocket Points: An app that rewards college students    for staying off their phones while in class
  • ● SafeDrive & Foynt: Two safe driving apps that    function in a similar way to DriveVoya but do not target    Students or Educators

Competitive Analysis*

*This analysis is based off of the Existing DriveVoya Website, which consisted of a single page with a field to submit your email address in the middle.

User Groups

We defined the following primary user groups based off of our user research:

  • ● Sponsors - individuals and companies that are going to be paying money to sponsor a school's fundraising efforts
  • ● Vendors - businesses willing to offer goods and services at a discount for students' safemiles
  • ● School Administrators - individuals with the ability to add and remove students from the app, who also regulate the    content posted in the Voya View

Secondary Users: Parents/Community Members and Students
While these are the primary users of the DriveVoya app, I determined that parents, community members, and students would be accessing the DriveVoya Website far less frequently than sponsors, vendors, and school administrators.

Primary Persona

The user interviews yielded similar results across the 3 user types. Though the information they are looking for is different, their goals and painpoints are the same:

  • ●  They hate having to dig around through multiple pages for the information      that they need
  • ●  They want to see actual facts and figures, not just promises
  • ●  They want to talk with a real person before their respective schools,      businesses, and companies can get involved with any particular school      fundraisers
  • ●  They feel uncomfortable giving out certain information when filling out online      forms

With this information in mind, I created one primary persona for the DriveVoya website: Simon, the Sponsor

UX Vision

As a team, we decided that design should:

  • 1.  Provide the 3 user groups with meaningful statistics about      the serious issue of distracted driving and give information      on how the app works
  • 2.  Promote the product by outlining DriveVoya's mission
  • 3.  Give clear steps for how the user could implement the      DriveVoya app into their life
